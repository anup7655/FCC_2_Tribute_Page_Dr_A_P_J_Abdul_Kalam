Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Here's a timeline of Dr. Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born on October 15, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954 - Graduates in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955 - Joins the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist.
  • 1960 - Receives his degree in Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1963 - Completes his master's degree in Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969 - Joins the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • 1970 - Assumes the role of Project Director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) and successfully deploys the Rohini satellite in orbit.
  • 1980 - Takes charge as the Chief Executive of Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).
  • 1992 - Becomes the Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1998 - Serves as the Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India.
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007.
  • 2015 - Passes away on July 27, leaving behind a legacy of scientific and educational contributions.
